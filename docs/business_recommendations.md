# Business Recommendations
## Executive Summary
CrediSphere Finance should use a balanced credit-risk strategy that reduces expected losses while preserving responsible loan growth.
The project identifies high-risk loan pools, expected credit loss concentration, delinquency movement, and collections priorities.
## Key Recommendations
### 1. Use Balanced Risk-Return Policy for New Originations
The Balanced Risk-Return Policy offers a strong trade-off between approval rate and default risk.
Recommended action:
- Avoid overly aggressive expansion policies.
- Avoid overly conservative policies that reject too many potentially good borrowers.
- Use policy thresholds as a strategy tool, not a fixed rule.
### 2. Prioritize Critical and High Model-Risk Loans
Critical and High risk loans create disproportionate expected credit loss.
Recommended action:
- Create a dedicated monitoring queue.
- Review high-value risky loans weekly.
- Trigger early-warning contact before severe delinquency.
### 3. Strengthen PAR30 Monitoring
PAR30 is an early warning signal for portfolio stress.
Recommended action:
- Track PAR30 by product, state, channel, and risk band.
- Investigate sudden regional or product-level spikes.
- Use PAR30 movement to adjust collections staffing.
### 4. Improve Collections Prioritization
Collections should not contact every overdue borrower equally.
Recommended action:
- Rank accounts by collections priority score.
- Combine days past due, outstanding balance, predicted default risk, promise-to-pay history, and recoverable value.
- Use different actions for early, mid, and late delinquency.
### 5. Monitor Regional Risk Concentration
The synthetic portfolio includes regional risk differences and a delinquency spike.
Recommended action:
- Review underwriting policy by state.
- Compare regional default rate and expected credit loss.
- Investigate root causes before tightening policy.
### 6. Track Responsible Lending Signals
Fairness checks should be used for monitoring, not as direct decision rules.
Recommended action:
- Compare approval and default outcomes across available groups.
- Avoid using sensitive attributes as direct approval criteria.
- Add governance notes to leadership reporting.
## Leadership Decision Framework
Use the dashboard and marts to answer:
- Should approval policy become stricter?
- Which segments create the highest loss exposure?
- Which customers should collections contact first?
- Which products or regions need monitoring?
- Can expected loss be reduced without cutting too much growth?
## Disclaimer
All data is synthetic and created for portfolio demonstration. These recommendations are based on simulated data and are not real-world lending advice.
